Detailed Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Our meticulous, step-by-step production protocols demonstrate our commitment to quality and consistency—the foundation of our brand's credibility.

Media & Broth Preparation Guide

Pikovskaya's Broth (for PSB)

Composition per 100L:

  • Glucose: 1 kg
  • Tricalcium Phosphate: 0.5 kg
  • Ammonium Sulphate: 0.05 kg
  • Potassium Chloride: 0.02 kg
  • Magnesium Sulphate: 0.01 kg
  • Yeast Extract: 0.05 kg
  • RO Water: to make 100L

Jensen's Medium (for Azotobacter)

Composition per 100L:

  • Sucrose: 2 kg
  • Dipotassium Phosphate: 0.1 kg
  • Magnesium Sulphate: 0.05 kg
  • Sodium Chloride: 0.05 kg
  • Ferrous Sulphate: 0.01 kg
  • Calcium Carbonate: 0.2 kg
  • RO Water: to make 100L

Aleksandrov Medium (for KMB)

Composition per 100L:

  • Glucose: 0.5 kg
  • Magnesium Sulphate: 0.05 kg
  • Ferric Chloride: Trace
  • Calcium Carbonate: 0.01 kg
  • Feldspar/Mica Powder (Potassium Source): 0.2 kg
  • RO Water: to make 100L

King's B Medium (for Pseudomonas)

Composition per 100L:

  • Proteose Peptone: 2 kg
  • Dipotassium Phosphate: 0.15 kg
  • Magnesium Sulphate: 0.15 kg
  • Glycerol: 1.5 L
  • RO Water: to make 100L

Potato Dextrose Broth (for Fungi)

  1. Wash and slice (unpeeled) 20 kg of potatoes.
  2. Boil in 50L of water for 30 minutes.
  3. Filter the broth through cheesecloth, collecting the liquid infusion.
  4. Add 2 kg of Dextrose (Glucose) to the infusion.
  5. Add RO Water to bring the total volume up to 100L and adjust pH.

Bio-Fertilizer Production SOPs

SOP: Liquid Bio-Fertilizers (PSB, KMB, Azotobacter)

  1. Media Preparation: Prepare a 4000L batch of the respective medium (Pikovskaya's, Aleksandrov, or Jensen's).
  2. Sterilization: Transfer media to the 5000L bioreactor. Sterilize at 121°C and 15 psi. Cool to 30°C.
  3. Inoculation: Aseptically transfer active starter culture from the seed bioreactor.
  4. Fermentation: Run for 48-96 hours while maintaining optimal temperature, pH, and aeration.
  5. Quality Check: Ensure CFU count is at least 5 x 10⁸ cells/ml and check for contamination.
  6. Packaging: Mix in stabilizers and pack using the automated liquid filling line.

SOP: VAM Mycorrhiza (Powder)

  1. Host Plant Cultivation: Grow host plants like maize in trays with sterilized vermiculite.
  2. Inoculation: Inoculate the vermiculite with pure VAM mother culture.
  3. Growth Period: Allow plants to grow for 60-90 days for fungi to colonize the roots.
  4. Harvesting & Processing: Uproot plants, collect root mass with substrate, air-dry, and blend.
  5. QC & Packaging: Test for infective propagule count (target: 100 IP/g). Pack into 1 Kg pouches.

Bio-Control Agent Production SOPs

SOP: Fungal Agents (Trichoderma, Beauveria, Metarhizium)

  1. Fermentation: Ferment the respective fungus in Potato Dextrose Broth (PDB) for 7-10 days.
  2. Carrier Prep & Blending: Spray the harvested broth onto sterilized Talc Powder in the 1 Tonne Ribbon Blender with 1% CMC.
  3. Drying & Milling: Dry the mixture until moisture is below 8%, then mill to a fine consistency.
  4. QC & Packaging: Final QC check for target CFU count (e.g., Trichoderma: 2 x 10⁶ cells/g), then pack.

SOP: Bacterial Agents (Pseudomonas fluorescens)

  1. Fermentation: Ferment *Pseudomonas fluorescens* in King's B Broth for 48-72 hours.
  2. Carrier Blending: Follow the same procedure as fungal agents, blending the liquid culture with sterilized talc powder.
  3. Drying & Packaging: Dry, mill, perform QC for target CFU count (2 x 10⁸ cells/g), and pack.

Growth Promoter & Nutrient SOPs

SOP: Seaweed Extract

  1. Raw Material: Procure high-quality dried *Ascophyllum nodosum* seaweed.
  2. Extraction: Perform alkaline extraction in a large mixing tank with potassium hydroxide solution under controlled temperature.
  3. Filtration & Concentration: Filter the extract to remove solids and concentrate it to the desired percentage.
  4. QC & Packaging: Check for specific gravity and nutrient profile, then bottle using the liquid filling line.

SOP: Humic Acid & Amino Acid

  1. Process: These are produced via alkaline extraction (Humic from Lignite) or enzymatic hydrolysis (Amino from Soy).
  2. Reaction: The process is carried out in dedicated mixing tanks/reactors.
  3. Formulation: The final extract/hydrolysate is filtered, stabilized, and formulated to the required concentration.
  4. QC & Packaging: Check for active ingredient percentage, pH, and specific gravity before bottling.

SOP: Chelated Zinc

  1. Reaction: React Zinc Sulphate with a chelating agent (e.g., EDTA) in the Glass-Lined Reactor with RO water.
  2. Drying: Transfer the resulting solution to a spray dryer to produce a fine, water-soluble powder.
  3. QC & Packaging: Test for zinc content and solubility before packaging into pouches.

Channel Partner Empowerment (Push Strategy)

  • Attractive Margins: Offer competitive, volume-based margins at each level of the supply chain to ensure channel loyalty and motivation.
  • Marketing & Sales Support: Provide partners with marketing materials, product training, and joint promotional activities. A dedicated field team will support distributors.
  • Credit & Logistics: Establish clear credit policies and partner with reliable logistics providers to ensure timely delivery and availability of stock.
  • Exclusive Territories: Appoint distributors with clearly defined territories to prevent channel conflict and encourage focused market development.

Farmer Engagement & Demand Generation (Pull Strategy)

  • Field Demonstrations: Conduct extensive on-farm demonstrations to showcase product efficacy and ROI in local conditions and on key crops.
  • Village-Level Meetings: Organize farmer meetings and workshops to educate them on the benefits of biological inputs and sustainable practices.
  • Digital Outreach: Utilize social media and platforms like WhatsApp to share testimonials, crop advisories, and product information in regional languages.
  • Retailer-Point Promotions: Run targeted promotions and offer combo-packs at the retail level to encourage trials and build brand recognition.

Scaling & Replication

Leadership & Diversification
